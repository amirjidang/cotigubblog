    The Founding of Boo Boo Goo

    Boo Boo Goo, a revolutionary product, was brought to life by the creative minds of Kiowa Kavoit, a 6-year-old girl, and her father, Andrew Kavovit. Together, they developed this unique solution for cuts and scrapes that would make healing a more enjoyable experience. Unlike traditional band-aids, Boo Boo Goo is a colored, paint-on bandage that comes in vibrant hues, making it a fun alternative for kids and adults alike.

    What sets Boo Boo Goo apart is not just its vibrant colors, but also its eco-friendly approach. The founders wanted to create a product that not only made healing fun but also minimized environmental impact. With this vision in mind, they carefully selected natural ingredients to formulate Boo Boo Goo.

    Boo Boo Goo is a non-flammable product that prioritizes the safety and well-being of its users. The founders consulted with physicians specializing in skin and wounds to ensure that the product would effectively protect and promote healing for various types of injuries.

    To showcase the variety of Boo Boo Goo colors and skin tones available, the founders extensively tested different shades. This attention to detail highlights their commitment to inclusivity. Boo Boo Goo truly caters to everyone, ensuring that individuals of all skin tones can find a bandage that matches their unique complexion.

    Boo Boo Goo brings a splash of color to the healing process, creating a positive and enjoyable experience for those with cuts and scrapes.

    Boo Boo Goo on Shark Tank

    During their captivating pitch on Shark Tank, Kiowa and Andrew presented a video demonstration showcasing the unique properties and functionality of Boo Boo Goo. The Sharks were intrigued by the concept and provided valuable feedback on the product. While Mark Cuban and Barbara Corcoran expressed reservations about the market potential, the other Sharks recognized the innovative nature of Boo Boo Goo.

    “Boo Boo Goo is a game-changer in the band-aid industry. It offers a fun and eco-friendly alternative to traditional bandages.” – Kevin O’Leary, Shark Tank Investor

    “I see great potential in Boo Boo Goo and believe it can revolutionize how we treat minor cuts and scrapes.” – Lori Greiner, Shark Tank Investor

    Kiowa and Andrew’s passion and enthusiasm for their product resonated with the Sharks, particularly with Kevin O’Leary. He made an offer of $100,000 for 25% equity in Boo Boo Goo, contingent upon the clearing of the pending patent. Recognizing the significance of the opportunity, Kiowa and Andrew accepted the deal, marking a pivotal moment for their business on the Shark Tank stage.

    Appearing on Shark Tank not only secured the much-needed investment for Boo Boo Goo but also provided the product with a significant boost in attention and publicity.     Post Shark Tank Success

    After their appearance on Shark Tank, Kiowa and Andrew experienced a surge in demand for Boo Boo Goo. The exposure on the show led to a significant number of orders for the product.

    Seeking to capitalize on their newfound success, the founders entered into negotiations with a major band-aid company to expand their product line. They aimed to introduce additional offerings such as BooBoo Bites, BooBoo Burns, BooBoo Salve, and BooBoo Block.

    Unfortunately, the name of the company they partnered with has not been revealed. However, it appears that they encountered challenges in fulfilling the orders, leading to complications in their business operations. Ultimately, the company went out of business, and Boo Boo Goo was no longer available.
