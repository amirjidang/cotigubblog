    Al McCoy Phoenix Suns

    Al has worked as the radio broadcaster for the Phoenix Suns since 1972 and earned the nickname The Voice of the Suns. Furthermore, he has only missed one match against Chicago Bulls on Dec 31, 2005, due to illness.

    Moreover, over the three decades he has stayed at the Suns, he has developed catchphrases like Shazam, Oh Brother, Heartbreak Hotel, Wham Bam Slam, Whammo, and Swish-a-roo for two.

    Additionally, McCoy is the 15th member of the Phoenix Suns Ring of Honor. McCoy was inducted to the Phoenix Suns Ring of Honor during half time break against Oklahoma City Thunder on March 3, 2017.

    McCoy currently serves as the senior vice president of broadcasting for the Phoenix Suns.

    Al McCoy Career

    Immediately after graduating from Drake University, McCoy worked at KJFJ radio in Webster City, LA. He worked at KJFJ in 1951. Additionally, McCoy worked at WHO (AM) as a radio host for three years. Al also worked as a broadcaster for the Triple-A Phoenix Giants baseball club.
