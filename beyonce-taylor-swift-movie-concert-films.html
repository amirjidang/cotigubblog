    The live music industry is a multi-billion dollar behemoth, and concert films have become a lucrative way for musicians to expand their reach and earn additional revenue.

    These cinematic captures of live performances offer fans a front-row seat to a concert's electrifying energy and camaraderie, even when they couldn't attend the show in person.

    In 2023 alone, two concert films joined the top ten-grossing concert films at the domestic box office. Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour concert film has become the highest-grossing concert film of all time, earning $178 million domestically since its debut in October 2023.

    Beyoncé's Renaissance: A Film By Beyoncé, which was released in December 2023, also joined the list, grossing over $21 million domestically in its first week.

    The film, directed and produced by Beyoncé herself, offers an intimate look at the creation and execution of her Renaissance World Tour, in support of her seventh studio Grammy-winning album Renaissance, released in 2022.

    Updated, April 2024: Following the release of Beyonce's album Cowboy Carter, Time magazine published an April 4th article, reporting that fans half-jokingly begged the singer for time to "financially recover" before the singer launched a tour in support of it:

    "As rumors fly, fans are begging Beyoncé to hold off on announcing a tour, joking that they still need to financially recover from her last one. The Renaissance World Tour, which wrapped up in Oct. 2023 and was her first since the On The Run Tour II in 2018.

    "According to Billboard, tickets for the Renaissance Tour ranged in price depending on the reseller. The average fan could expect to pay around $400 to $550, Billboard reported, with resale tickets for her Houston show going for as low as $279."

    As the outlet noted, no official announcement regarding a Cowboy Carter tour had yet been made.

    10 Glee: The 3D Concert Movie

    Glee: The 3D Concert Movie's Domestic Gross: $11.9 Million

    In 2011, the hit musical TV show Glee brought its riveting energy to the big screen with Glee: The 3D Concert Movie.

    The film, directed by Kevin Tancharoen and Jennifer Arnold, captured the electrifying performances of the show's stars, including Lea Michele, Chris Colfer, Cory Monteith, and Dianna Agron, as they embarked on their Glee Live! In Concert! summer tour.

    It featured behind-the-scenes footage and a set list of songs from the show's first and second seasons.

    Despite concerns that fans might not be willing to pay to see the same performances they could watch for free on television, the concert film proved to be a commercial success, grossing over $11.9 million domestically.

    9 Madonna: Truth Or Dare

    Madonna: Truth Or Dare's Domestic Gross: $15 Million

    The Queen of Pop unveiled an unprecedented look into her life with the epic documentary concert film Madonna: Truth or Dare.

    Directed by Alek Keshishian, the film offered an unfiltered and intimate glimpse into Madonna's world on and off-stage, as she embarked on her controversial Blond Ambition World Tour.

    8 Jonas Brothers: The 3D Concert Experience

    Jonas Brothers: The 3D Concert Experience's Domestic Gross: $19.1 Million

    The Jonas Brothers, heartthrobs of the teen and tween demographic, captured the whirlwind of their meteoric rise to fame in the concert film Jonas Brothers: The 3D Concert Experience.

    Directed by Bruce Hendricks, the film chronicled the trio's Burning Up tour, showcasing their electrifying live performances and boyish charm, as well as documentary footage on the lives of the three brothers.

    The film's release coincided with the Jonas Brothers' peak popularity, with their music topping charts and their faces gracing magazine covers worldwide.

    Although the film was rocked by negative reviews from critics, the band's fan base ate it up at the box office.

    It grossed over $19.1 million domestically, which places it among the top-grossing concert films.

    6 Katy Perry: Part Of Me

    Katy Perry: Part Of Me's Domestic Gross: $25.3 Million

    Leave it to Katy Perry to light up the stage; the Queen of Camp released her concert film Katy Perry: Part Of Me in 2021, which offered a closer glimpse into the life and career of the pop superstar during her highly successful California Dreams World Tour.

    Directed by Dan Cutforth and Jane Lipsitz, the film seamlessly blends concert footage, behind-the-scenes moments, and candid interviews, providing a comprehensive portrait of Perry as an artist and a person.

    Released in the wake of Perry's record-breaking album Teenage Dream – which tied with Michael Jackson's Bad for the most number one singles on one album – Katy Perry: Part of Me capitalized on Perry's immense popularity and struck a chord with audiences worldwide.

    The film's financial success, grossing over $25.3 million domestically, reflected Perry's appeal as a pop culture icon.

    3 Michael Jackson’s This Is It

    Michael Jackson’s This Is It's Domestic Gross: $72.1 Million

    Of course, the greatest entertainer of all time will undoubtedly feature in a list that has to do with entertainment.

    This Is It was released just four months after Michael Jackson's untimely death in 2009:

    The concert film offered a poignant and intimate glimpse into the King of Pop's final preparations for his long-anticipated This Is It residency at London's O2 Arena.

    This compelling documentary, directed by Kenny Ortega, went beyond a traditional concert film, delving into Jackson's meticulous process and dedication to his craft.

    The film comprises interviews, dancer auditions, rehearsals, backstage footage, and groundbreaking technical elements planned for the never-to-be-seen live shows.

    This Is It received generally positive reviews from critics and Jackson fans, but criticism arose due to rumors that the film was made to profit from Jackson's death.

    The controversy led some fans to boycott the movie. Nevertheless, the film earned $72 million at domestic theaters.

    According to Guinness World Records, This Is It is the highest-grossing documentary of all time.

    2 Justin Bieber: Never Say Never

    Justin Bieber: Never Say Never's Domestic Gross: $73 Million

    Justin Bieber: Never Say Never centered on the then-teen pop sensation Justin Bieber.

    Directed by Jon M. Chu, the film interspersed Bieber's concert footage from his 2010 tour with intimate behind-the-scenes moments, offering a closer look into Bieber's journey from small-town Canadian boy to global pop icon.

    The film also featured Boyz II Men, Miley Cyrus, Sean Kingston, and Snoop Dogg.

    Justin Bieber: Never Say Never resonated deeply with Bieber's large fan base, affectionately known as "Beliebers," which helped it gross over $73 million domestically.

    The film's success was further fueled by the accompanying EP, Never Say Never: The Remixes, which topped the Billboard 200 chart.

    1 Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour

    Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour's Domestic Gross: $178.9 Million

    Taylor Swift's The Eras Tour concert film, released in 2023, is a cultural phenomenon, shattering records and solidifying its place as the highest-grossing concert film of all time.

    Filmed over Swift's first three shows at SoFi Stadium in Inglewood, California, The Eras Tour highlighted an extensive set list, featuring nearly all songs from the tour's standard repertoire.

    The film's meticulous editing, condensed to under three hours, did not diminish the grandeur of the live experience, but instead intensified its impact:

    Swift struck an unprecedented distribution agreement with theaters for the film, bypassing the traditional producer–distributor–exhibitor model of releasing films.

    The film's unorthodox release strategy generated much commentary and controversy. Swift's dedicated fanbase, known as "Swifties," flocked to theaters in droves to relive the tour's magic and witness their idol's spellbinding stage presence on the big screen.

    The film's domestic gross of $178.9 million reflects the immense popularity of Taylor Swift and the enduring appeal of her music.
