    Grammy Awards Best American Roots Song Winner History

    The Grammy Award for Best American Roots Song is a prestigious category at the annual Grammy Awards ceremony. It was established in 2014 to recognize quality songwriting in the American Roots subgenres, including folk, bluegrass, and regional roots music.

    22 Aug 2024, 00:00

    The Grammy Award for Best American Roots Song is a prestigious category at the annual Grammy Awards ceremony. It was established in 2014 to recognize quality songwriting in the American Roots subgenres, including folk, bluegrass, and regional roots music.

    This award is presented by the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences (NARAS) and honors outstanding songwriters in the American Roots Music genres. The award goes to the songwriters of the winning song, not the performing artists.

    The first recipient of this award was Edie Brickell and Steve Martin in 2014, and the current holder is Bonnie Raitt for the song “Just Like That” in 2023. Jason Isbell is the only person to have won this award more than once, with two wins.
