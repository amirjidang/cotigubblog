    Linda Gray net worth and salary: Linda Gray is an American actress who has a net worth of $12 million. Linda is probably best-known for appearing as Sue Ellen Ewing in several iterations of television show “Dallas”. The role earned Linda several Golden Globe and Emmy nominations. She appeared in 308 episodes of the series’ original run and 40 episodes of the reboot from 2012 to 2014. Between 1994 and 1995 she appeared in 29 episodes of “Models Inc.”.

    Linda Ann Gray was born in Santa Monica, California on September 12, 1940. Linda Gray began her professional career as a model in the early 60s. Her modeling work led to a very successful career in commercials. She did not make the transition to network television until ten years later, in 1974, when she was cast in a guest-starring role on “Marcus Welby, M.D.”. This led to more television work and she appeared in guest-starring or co-starring roles in such television projects as “McCloud”, “All That Glitters”, “Murder in Peyton Place”, and “The Two Worlds of Jennie Logan”.

    She became a household name for her performance as Sue Ellen Ewing, on the long-running evening soap, “Dallas”. Throughout the 90s, she appeared as a guest-star on multiple shows, including “Bonanza: The Return”, “Melrose Place”, and “The Bold and the Beautiful”. She recently returned to the role that made her famous, appearing on the series continuation of “Dallas”.

    Personal Life: Linda was married to artist Ed Thrasher from 1962 to 1983. They had two children together. Ed’s sister Marilyn Thrasher is the mother of “Bionic Woman” actress Lindsay Wagner. So, Linda is technically Lindsay’s aunt.

    Real Estate: In 1971 Linda and Ed paid $3 million for an impressive, nearly 3-acre, property in Santa Clarity, California. Over the next several years they performed significant renovations, tapping architect A. Quincey Jones. Linda listed the home for sale in 2018 for just under $3 million. She did not find a buyer and ultimately removed the listing. She still owns this property as of this writing.

    In 1981 Linda paid $590,000 for a ranch-style home in the Beverly Hills Post Office neighborhood (technically not in Beverly Hills but serviced their postal workers and fire/police). In 2011 she sold this home for $2.7 million.
