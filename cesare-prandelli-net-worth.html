    Cesare Prandelli is an Italian football coach and former football player with an estimated net worth of $20 million.

    As a football player, Cesare Prandelli started his career in 1974, and eventually, he joined Cremonese. He then played for Atalanta between 1978-1979 and 1985-1990. Also, he played for Juventus from 1979 to 1985.

    His coaching career began with Atalanta youth team from 1990 to 1997. Prandelli also served as the team's caretaker from November 1993 to June 1994 for the first team and relegated to Serie B. He had a brief stint as a coach for Lecce in 1997-1998.

    Other teams that Prandelli managed include Verona, Venezia, Parma, Roma, Florentina, and Galatasaray. When he was with Florentina, he was chosen as the Serie A Coach of the Year for 2008-2009 season. Also, he coached the Italian national team from 2010-2014.

    Cesare Claudio Prandelli was born on August 19, 1957 in Orzinuovi, Brescia, Italy.
