    Family, Friends, and Community Mourns the Loss of its Cherished Member, Cody Ring

    Cody Ring, a former Special Handling Air Cargo at the United States Air Force, died unexpectedly on Monday, December 11, 2023, leaving family and friends profoundly aggrieved and community grappling with shock and grief. Our thoughts and prayers are with the family and friends during this challenging time.

    Who was Cody Ring?

    Cody Ring, affectionately known as Cub, was a brilliant woman who had served her country with her dedication. She was a former Special Handling Air Cargo at the United States Air Force from December 1984 to February 1992. She was appointed to heavy equipment operation, including paperwork and computer-generated reports, at the 21st headquarters in Langley, Virginia. She was also assigned with training and overseeing airmen after she made NCO.

    She was committed to helping others and serving her community to the best of her ability. She studied Emergency Medical Services at Blue Mountain Community College from 1992 to 1993, a testament to her dedication to serving her nation. She also studied at the Community College of the Air Force.

    What happened to Cody’s ring?

    Cody Ring passed away unexpectedly on Monday, December 11, 2023. The details of her untimely demise have not been made public. Her friend Gail Wheeler shared the tragic news of her passing on her social media.

    Cody had been sick and was struggling with health-related issues for some time. According to another friend of hers, Cody’s condition worsened, resulting in organ failure, and she passed away.

    Her untimely death has robbed the world of a brilliant human being who had devoted her life to the service of the community. She strived to make the world a better place every waking moment of her life. She was known for her kindness and generosity and had forged lasting bonds with everyone in her community with her warmth and positivity.
